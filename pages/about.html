Our Story

The Compassion Project Inc. was born from one Thanksgiving morning and a calling that wouldn’t quiet down.

Four years ago, my family wasn’t going to be around for Thanksgiving, and a dear friend invited me to help prepare meals and care packages for people experiencing homelessness and hardship. Volunteering and giving back had always been a passion of mine, but that morning changed everything. That’s when I met Leanna. Hearing her story, seeing her heart, and watching her serve with such purpose lit something inside me. I knew I wanted to do more and that somehow, this was where I was meant to be.

Finding Purpose

The following year, a few weeks before Thanksgiving, I felt a strong and unmistakable pull to do more than just show up. I launched a GoFundMe, shared it with friends and family, and within just five days, we raised $1,700 which was enough to fill my SUV with gloves, hats, scarves, first aid kits, hygiene items, and other essentials. That Thanksgiving morning, I showed up unannounced with the packed car, and Leanna told me she’d been heartbroken because they hadn’t raised enough funds for the care packages that year. She worried they’d be empty but as we unloaded every box, we both knew that calling had been for a reason.

The next year, we set our sights higher. Our $4,000 goal became $5,600 raised in just a few weeks and that’s when I realized this wasn’t just a seasonal project anymore. It was something deeper. Something that needed a home.

From left to right: Leanna Chroman, Kara Tilford, Megan Rabb, Henry Tilford

Turning Compassion Into Action

In the summer of 2025, I felt that same inner voice urging me to take the next step. I began researching how to start a nonprofit, gathering a small board of people who believed in the same mission, to put compassion into action and make a lasting impact year-round. Within weeks, The Compassion Project Inc. was born, and soon after, we received our official 501(c)(3) determination from the IRS.

Our mission now goes beyond providing comfort during the holidays. We’re creating programs that meet immediate needs such as meals, clothing, care packages, and financial relief while also building partnerships and opportunities that strengthen our community all year long.

Why We Do What We Do

  • Because compassion shouldn’t be limited to one day of the year.
  • Because everyone deserves to feel seen, supported, and valued.
  • Because sometimes, the smallest act of kindness can change everything.

The Compassion Project exists to remind our neighbors that hope still lives here, and it starts with each of us.

Our Mission, Vision & Core Values

Mission

The Compassion Project Inc. is dedicated to addressing the immediate needs of individuals and families facing hardship. Through the delivery of meals, clothing, and care packages, we transform compassion into action and empower our community with support and dignity.

Vision

We envision a thriving community strengthened by compassion, where individuals, families, and organizations work together to meet needs, uplift one another, and grow through shared support and connection.

Our Core Values

Compassion

Every act of kindness matters.

Dignity

We serve with respect, empathy, and care.

Integrity

We are transparent and accountable in all we do.

Community

We believe in the power of people helping people.

Action

Compassion is more than a feeling; it’s what we do.
