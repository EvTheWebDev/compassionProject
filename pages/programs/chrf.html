Compassion Hardship Relief Program

Timely Support for Unexpected Life Events

Overview

The Hardship Relief Program provides direct financial and essential support to individuals and families in Central Indiana who are experiencing sudden hardship. Whether it’s a job loss, medical emergency, house fire, or other unexpected life event, this program is designed to step in quickly and offer relief, stability, and hope.

Purpose

To provide compassionate, timely assistance to neighbors facing immediate challenges, helping them regain stability while maintaining dignity and connection within the community.

Our Goal

To ensure no neighbor faces hardship alone. Through compassion, community, and collaboration, we aim to meet needs quickly and help individuals move forward with renewed strength and hope.

Eligibility

  • Must reside within The Compassion Project’s Central Indiana service area.
  • Must be facing an unexpected or emergency hardship that has created a financial or essential need.
  • Must complete a hardship application or be referred by a community member.

Examples of Assistance

  • Temporary financial relief
  • Groceries, hygiene kits, or clothing
  • Emergency care packages
  • Gift cards for gas or essential items
  • Coordinated fundraising support for verified hardship

How It Works: Application & Referral

  1. Apply or Refer: Individuals may apply directly through our online application form. Community members or local organizations may also refer someone in need.
  2. Review Process: Applications are reviewed by the Board of Directors or an appointed committee based on urgency, financial need, and available resources.
  3. Support Determination: Assistance may include direct financial aid, essential items, or other resources. Funds are provided directly to the recipient unless otherwise approved.

Funding and Follow-Up

Funding Source: This program is supported through general donations, community fundraising events, and dedicated giving campaigns. 100% of designated Hardship Relief funds go toward assisting recipients.

Follow-Up: Recipients may be contacted to ensure their immediate needs have been met and to connect them with other local support networks if needed.
